MORERA'S THEOREM

Let $f: \Omega \to \mathbb{C}$ be a continuous function defined on an open set $\Omega \subset \mathbb{C}$. Suppose that for every piecewise $C^1$ closed curve $\gamma \subset \Omega$, we have \[ \int_{\gamma} f(z)\,dz = 0. \] Fix $z_0 \in \Omega$ and define \[ F(z) = \int_{\gamma_{z_0,z}} f(w)\,dw, \] where $\gamma_{z_0,z}$ is any piecewise smooth path in $\Omega$ joining $z_0$ to $z$. The assumption implies that $F$ is well-defined, since the integral is path-independent.

Now let $h \to 0$ such that $z + h \in \Omega$. Then \[ \frac{F(z+h) - F(z)}{h} = \frac{1}{h} \int_{\gamma_{z,z+h}} f(w)\,dw. \] By continuity of $f$, we may write $f(w) = f(z) + \varepsilon(w)$, where $\varepsilon(w) \to 0$ as $w \to z$. Hence, \[ \frac{F(z+h) - F(z)}{h} = f(z) + \frac{1}{h} \int_{\gamma_{z,z+h}} \varepsilon(w)\,dw. \] The second term tends to $0$ as $h \to 0$, showing that $F'(z) = f(z)$. Therefore $f$ is holomorphic on $\Omega$.
